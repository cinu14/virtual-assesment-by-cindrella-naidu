** Abhishek's Question was if we can collect user's name, email id, company name, employee size without user form submission ** My Answer: **"Generally, no. We cannot directly collect a visitor's personal information such as their name, personal email address, phone number, or employee details without their consent, form submission, or authentication. This is restricted by browser security policies and privacy regulations like GDPR and CCPA.** **However, in B2B websites, we can use visitor identification and intent data tools to gather company-level information based on the visitor's IP address and behavioral data. Using tools such as Clearbit Reveal, 6sense, Leadfeeder, ZoomInfo, Demandbase, and Albacross, we can sometimes identify information like:** * Company name * Company domain * Industry * Company size (employee count) * Geographic location * Pages visited * Visitor behavior and engagement **For example, if a visitor from a corporate network visits our pricing or services page, these tools may identify that the visitor belongs to a specific organization, such as Microsoft or IBM, along with company-level attributes.** **However, these tools do not directly expose personally identifiable information like an individual's name or personal email address unless the user has previously submitted a form, logged in, consented to tracking, or is already present in a connected CRM or marketing automation platform.** **In my previous projects, we have integrated tools like Google Analytics, Google Tag Manager, Microsoft Clarity, HubSpot, and visitor intelligence platforms such as Clearbit and Leadfeeder to analyze visitor behavior and capture qualified B2B leads."**
